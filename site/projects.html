OASIS

The Operational Awareness Secure Information System is a Blue-Force Tracker / Information Sharing Device for military use. The design of the device was conducted as part of my Master of Engineering project at Cornell University from 2010-2011 with Jack Cheung, Jason Yen, and James Wei

The device's design consisted of a Texas Instruments OMAP ARM processor, tablet phone form-factor, and a SAASM GPS module.

Ghost-Writing Robot

This project consisted of using two Atmel ATMega32 microcontrollers (AVR) communicating with each other using 433MHz transcievers. The purpose of the project was to utilize a novel drivetrain consisting of a large steel ball (converted ball-bearing) as both a pivot and main driver.

Vehicle Dashboard

This vehicle dashboard moves away from the bulky, expensive designs that utilize analog gauges and leverages a digital tachometer consisting of an array of LEDs as well as a gear indicator 7-segment display.

The device is a latch-based design that gets information from the car's custom Engine Control Module, which was also designed in-house for the project. Physical features of the dashboard are the fiberglass shell and lexan screen which has been water-proofed to withstand wet Ithaca, NY weather.
